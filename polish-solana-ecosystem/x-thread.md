automation, privacy, liquidity, AI signals, and DePIN compute.

That is why I am watching @Tempo_Trade, @darklakefi, @deloraprotocol, @outlight_ai, and @sallar_io.

2/ The thesis:

Poland's Solana edge is execution infrastructure.

The most useful teams are not only asking "what can users click?"

They are asking "how do builders, traders, wallets, and agents execute better?"

3/ @Tempo_Trade is interesting because trading automation is more than bots.

It is programmable execution policy:

- when to act
- what risk to accept
- what liquidity to avoid
- when to stop

That matters on Solana because speed turns strategy into UX.

4/ @darklakefi points at a different execution problem: privacy.

As onchain markets get faster, information leakage becomes more expensive.

The next question is not only "was my swap cheap?"

It is "what did my swap reveal?"

5/ @deloraprotocol is focused on cross-chain liquidity and execution access.

That is a builder painkiller.

Wallets, agents, and apps should not need to become routing teams before they ship.

Unified liquidity access is a product primitive.

6/ @outlight_ai shows the AI side of the stack.

Crypto does not lack data. It lacks trustworthy filters.

The edge is turning social + onchain noise into ranked, explainable signals before the market has fully priced them.

7/ @sallar_io adds the DePIN angle.

If mobile devices can contribute useful compute and settle rewards on Solana, the contributor base gets much wider than GPU farms.

Small compute contributions need cheap coordination. That is a Solana-shaped problem.

8/ The stack I want to see:

Outlight-style signal ranking
Tempo-style automated policies
Delora-style liquidity access
Darklake-style private execution
Sallar-style distributed compute

That combination is agent-native infrastructure.

9/ Build idea:

An autonomous trading assistant that can:

- read AI-ranked market signals
- choose execution policies
- route liquidity across venues
- protect sensitive order flow
- publish verifiable work receipts on Solana

That is not sci-fi. It is a product roadmap.

10/ The Polish Solana scene is early, but the ingredients are strong.

If these teams keep shipping, Poland can own a sharp niche:

execution infrastructure for builders, traders, wallets, and agents.
